Pittal (Brass) (Copper + Zinc) 
Best For: Cooking (needs tin-coating), storing dry items, eating certain foods.
Benefits: Antibacterial, good heat conductor, provides zinc, builds immunity.
Caution: Avoid with sour/salty food as it can react. 
Kansa (Bronze) (Copper + Tin) 
Best For: Eating, drinking water, serving (thalis, bowls).
Benefits: Balances Pitta/Vata/Kapha, purifies water, improves digestion, boosts immunity, doesn't react with acidic foods.
Note: Often called the "hands of grace" in Ayurveda for its holistic benefits. 
Tambaa (Copper) (Pure Copper) 
Best For: Drinking water (store overnight).
Benefits: Anti-microbial, aids digestion, boosts immunity, excellent heat conductor.
Caution: Can react with acidic foods and sour items. 
Summary Recommendation:
Water: Tambaa (Copper) or Kansa (Bronze).
Eating/Serving: Kansa (Bronze) is ideal as it's non-reactive and balances doshas.
Cooking: Pittal (Brass) if tin-coated (kalai), but Kansa is also good and safer with all foods. 
Final Tip: Kansa is generally considered the most versatile and healthy choice for daily eating and water, while copper is great for water storage and brass for general cooking (with coating).
